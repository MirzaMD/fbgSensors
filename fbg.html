The Power of FBG Sensors in Cancer Detection

What are FBG Sensors?

Fiber Bragg Grating (FBG) sensors are optical sensors that use light to measure various physical parameters such as temperature, strain, and pressure. These sensors are gaining popularity in medical applications for their high sensitivity and precision.

Advantages in Medical Use

  • Non-invasive and highly sensitive
  • Immune to electromagnetic interference
  • Real-time data measurement
  • Cost-effective compared to traditional methods

FBG Sensors in Cancer Detection

FBG sensors can detect temperature variations in cancerous cells, making them a valuable tool in the early detection of breast and other cancers. The sensors provide real-time, precise thermal readings, helping identify tumor growth by monitoring temperature differences in tissues.

Key Points:
Challenges in Traditional Cancer Detection: Current methods like mammography, MRI, and biopsies are often invasive, expensive, or limited in sensitivity. Thereâ€™s a need for non-invasive, cost-effective tools for early cancer detection.

FBG Sensors for Medical Applications: FBG sensors, being fiberoptic-based, offer intelligent sensing capabilities. They can measure vital parameters like body temperature and blood pressure, which are essential for identifying early symptoms of diseases, including cancer.

Thermal Sensitivity in Cancer Cells: Tumor cells often exhibit higher temperatures due to faster metabolic rates and increased blood flow. FBG sensors can be used as thermal sensors to detect these temperature changes with high precision, which is critical for early cancer detection, especially for cancers like breast cancer.

Advantages of FBG Sensors:

  • Immune to electromagnetic interference.
  • Low power consumption.
  • Ability to be multiplexed into sensor networks.
  • Non-invasive and real-time data provision.
Challenges: Despite their promise, FBG sensors face issues such as a low signal-to-noise ratio, cross-sensitivity, and environmental interference. Optimization of the Q-factor is crucial for their performance in cancer detection.
